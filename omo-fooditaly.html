2.1.2. A

  • Here are ten notable Italian food books that have been widely recognized for their contributions to Italian culinary literature:
  • Essentials of Classic Italian Cooking by Marcella Hazan: A comprehensive guide combining her earlier works, offering a foundational approach to Italian cooking with reliable and authentic recipes
  • The Silver Spoon: Originally published in 1950, this cookbook is a classic reference for Italian cuisine with over 2,000 recipes and lavish photography, suitable for both beginners and experienced chefs
  • Italian Food by Elizabeth David: A pioneering book that introduced postwar Europe to the rich flavors of Italian cuisine, emphasizing the importance of quality ingredients and traditional methods
  • La Cucina: The Regional Cooking of Italy by The Italian Academy of Cuisine: A result of extensive research documenting traditional Italian recipes from home cooks across the country, highlighting regional diversity
  • Bastianich's Italian 101 by Lidia Matticchio Bastianich: A user-friendly cookbook with well-written recipes and a focus on authentic Italian dishes, including a glossary and index of ingredients
  • The Food of Italy by Claudia Roden: An encyclopedic work that captures the breadth of Italian cuisine with regional recipes, country cooking, and grand dishes, reflecting Roden's deep knowledge and passion
  • My Simple Italian by Theo Randall: Offers over 100 straightforward recipes using high-quality ingredients for home cooks, showcasing Randall's favorite Italian dishes
  • Pellegrino Artusi’s Science in the Kitchen and the Art of Eating Well: A historical cookbook dating back to 1891, providing insight into traditional Italian cooking with nearly 800 recipes, some of which have stood the test of time
  • Venice: Four Seasons of Home Cooking by Russell Norman: Explores Venetian home cooking with 130 accessible recipes and beautiful photography, along with insights into local markets and suppliers
  • Amaretto, Apple Cake and Artichokes by Anna del Conte: A collection of beautiful recipes and personal anecdotes that delve into the heart of Italian cooking, from classic dishes to more unusual ones
  • These books provide a rich tapestry of Italian culinary tradition and innovation, catering to a range of cooking skills and interests.
